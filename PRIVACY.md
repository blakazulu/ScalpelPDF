# Scalpel PDF — Privacy Policy

_Last updated: June 21, 2026_

Scalpel PDF ("Scalpel", "the app") is a local, portable PDF editor for Windows. This policy explains what data the app handles. The short version: **Scalpel collects nothing, transmits nothing, and contains no telemetry, ads, or accounts.**

## Data we collect

**None.** Scalpel does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information to the developer or any third party. There are no analytics, no crash-reporting uploads, no advertising identifiers, and no user accounts.

## How your files are handled

Every PDF you open is processed **entirely on your own device**. Files are never uploaded to any server. Editing, annotating, merging, splitting, signing, form-filling, and flattening all happen locally. Scalpel works fully offline.

## Data stored on your device

Scalpel saves a small amount of data locally on your computer so the app can function and remember your preferences:

- **Settings** (theme, language, view preferences) — stored in the Windows registry under `HKCU\Software\Scalpel` (virtualized per-package when installed from the Microsoft Store).
- **Saved signatures** — stored locally in your user app-data folder so you can reuse them.
- **Temporary working files** — created while editing and cleaned up automatically.

This data never leaves your device and is removed when you uninstall the app.

## Network access

Scalpel does not require or use an internet connection to operate.

## Children's privacy

Scalpel does not collect any data from anyone, including children.
